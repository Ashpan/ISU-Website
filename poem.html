Poem Review


Survival by Iris Tennent is a poem the life of all kinds of animals, and how they live their everyday life. Tennent wrote this poem because she is a woman who loves all sorts of wildlife, nature, countryside, and most importantly, she does not condone and cannot stand cruelty (especially towards animals) of any kind. Below I have recorded the poem and overlayed images that contribute to the overall message and theme of the poem.






POEM LYRICS - SURVIVAL BY IRIS TENNENT

Fly high bird, fly high
away from man's lead death.
Your feathers scorched from bullets,
as you gasp your last breath. Run fox run,
your freedom depends on your speed,
as the hunters close in, they do not care how you bleed. Burrow, rabbit, burrow
dig as deep as you can.
The small beady eyes
and ferrets teeth,
you cannot hide from man. Swim, whale, swim
away from the sharp harpoon,
no peace for you
until the water is stained red
instead of blue. They call it sport,
but to an animal, or bird
trying to survive.
Life is hard enough, just to live.
